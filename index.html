About Apostille

  • Apostille is an international certification under the Hague Convention of 1961.
  • Recognized in over 120 member countries.
  • Issued by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), India.
  • Required for study abroad, employment, migration, or business.
  • It is a sticker or stamp affixed to the document.
  • Verifies authenticity without embassy legalization.
  • Accepted in USA, UK, France, Germany, Australia, Japan, etc.
  • Covers personal documents like birth, marriage, death certificates.
  • Covers educational documents like degrees, diplomas, transcripts.
  • Covers commercial documents like contracts and company papers.
  • Processing time is usually 2–5 working days.
  • Eliminates multiple layers of verification.
  • Needed for visa applications, higher education, and work permits.
  • Saves time and cost compared to embassy attestation.
  • Only valid if issued by the official MEA authority.

About Embassy Attestation

  • Required for non-Hague Convention countries.
  • Verification through the respective foreign embassy.
  • Mandatory for UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, China, etc.
  • Used for employment visas, family visas, and business setup.
  • Steps: State verification → MEA attestation → Embassy attestation.
  • Ensures acceptance by foreign governments.
  • Covers educational, personal, and commercial documents.
  • Includes medical certificates and police clearance certificates.
  • Processing time varies (7–15 working days).
  • More complex than apostille.
  • Needed for Middle East job contracts and migration.
  • Without embassy attestation, documents may be rejected abroad.
  • Some embassies require translation into local language.
  • Fees depend on embassy and document type.
  • Provides legal recognition in non-Hague countries.

Commonly Attested Certificates & Purposes

  • Educational: Degrees, diplomas, transcripts (study/work abroad).
  • Personal: Birth, marriage, death certificates (family visas, migration).
  • Commercial: Contracts, company registration papers (business setup).
  • Other: Police clearance, medical certificates (immigration/employment).
